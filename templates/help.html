Understanding Vehicle Specifications

This guide explains what each specification means when comparing vehicles.

Company

The manufacturer or brand of the vehicle. This indicates who builds the car, such as Toyota, Ford, BMW, etc.

Model

The specific model name of the vehicle within a manufacturer's lineup (e.g., Camry, F-150, 3 Series).

Horsepower

Engine power output measured in horsepower (hp). Typically shown as "XXX hp @ XXXX rpm".

What to look for: Higher horsepower means faster acceleration but lower fuel economy.

Torque

Rotational force (lb-ft) affecting acceleration and towing.

What to look for: High torque at low RPMs means better daily performance and towing.

Transmission Type

  • Automatic: Shifts gears automatically
  • Manual: Driver manually shifts gears
  • CVT: Continuously variable with no fixed gear ratios
  • Dual-clutch: Automated manual with rapid shifting

Drivetrain

  • FWD: Front-Wheel Drive
  • RWD: Rear-Wheel Drive
  • AWD: All-Wheel Drive
  • 4WD: Four-Wheel Drive (selectable)

Fuel Economy

Miles per gallon (MPG) in city and highway.

Format: "XX city/YY highway mpg"

What to look for: Higher MPG = lower fuel costs + better efficiency.

Number of Doors

Total doors including rear hatch (if applicable). Affects passenger access and cargo convenience.

Price

Listed as MSRP. May be a single value or a range like "$25,000 - $35,000".

Note: Real prices vary by dealer and location.

Model Year Range

The years during which the vehicle was available. E.g., "2018–2022" or "2021–present".

Body Type

  • Sedan: 4-door with a trunk
  • SUV: Sport Utility Vehicle
  • Truck: Pickup with cargo bed
  • Coupe: 2-door, sporty style
  • Hatchback: Rear hatch opens upward
  • Wagon: Extended cargo behind rear seats

Engine Type

  • Layout: V6, I4, V8, etc.
  • Displacement in liters
  • Tech features: turbo, hybrid, EV

Number of Cylinders

  • 3–4 cylinders: Fuel efficient
  • 6 cylinders: Balanced performance
  • 8+ cylinders: High power, lower efficiency

HP per $1000

This value shows how much horsepower you get per $1,000 spent.

Formula: Horsepower ÷ (Price ÷ 1000)

What to look for: Higher = better value for performance.

Visual Indicators

  • ★ = Best value indicator
  • Green highlight = Better value
  • Bold = Emphasizes winner in a metric

Tips for Comparison

  • Match car types (e.g., SUV vs SUV)
  • Use HP per $1000 for value comparison
  • Factor in your usage: city, off-road, family
  • Higher isn’t always better (e.g., price)
